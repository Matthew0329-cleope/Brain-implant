Transforming Neuropsychiatric Care

Pioneering ultrasonic neuromodulation technology to treat the untreatable

NeuroWave Technologies is at the forefront of non-invasive brain stimulation, developing groundbreaking ultrasound technology that offers new hope for patients with treatment-resistant neuropsychiatric conditions. Founded in 2018 by a team of neuroscientists, engineers, and clinicians from Imperial College London and Oxford University, we're on a mission to revolutionize mental health treatment through precision neuromodulation.

How It Works

The SONIC-Pulse™ system uses low-intensity focused ultrasound (LIFU) technology to modulate neural activity in targeted brain regions associated with mood regulation. The system consists of three key components:

  1. Implantable Device: A compact, subcutaneous implant placed under the scalp in a 45-minute outpatient procedure under local anesthesia.
  2. External Controller: A patient-friendly handheld device that wirelessly communicates with the implant to control treatment sessions.
  3. Physician Programming System: A sophisticated software platform that allows clinicians to personalize treatment parameters based on individual patient needs and response data.

The ultrasound waves generated by the device create subtle mechanical effects in the targeted brain regions, which modulate neural activity and promote neuroplasticity. This approach offers several advantages over traditional neurostimulation methods, including greater precision, minimal side effects, and the ability to reach deep brain structures without invasive surgery.

Ongoing Research & Clinical Trials

Our commitment to scientific rigor and patient safety drives our research program. We are currently conducting several clinical trials to evaluate the safety and efficacy of our technology:

  • SONIC-D Trial: A randomized controlled trial of the SONIC-Pulse™ system for treatment-resistant depression across five NHS centers in the UK.
  • WAVE-A Study: An investigational trial exploring applications for treatment-resistant anxiety disorders.
  • PULSE-OCD Pilot: A preliminary study evaluating ultrasonic stimulation for obsessive-compulsive disorder.

Additionally, our research and development team is exploring next-generation technologies to expand our therapeutic capabilities to additional neurological and psychiatric conditions.
